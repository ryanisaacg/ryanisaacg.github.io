Recurse Center: Week 4

29 Jul 2024

My focus in my fourth week was almost entirely on the Recurse Game Jam. I teamed up with Robin on her game puzdug[1], a roguelike puzzle dungeon in x86 assembly. She had set out to make a game that fit in the boot sector, so that it could be played bare-metal. We did briefly have a build that worked on a DOS-less Qemu instance, but there is a 512 byte limit for the boot sector which we quickly shot past. The final product was a whopping 1KB and included fog of war, player movement, enemies, a win and loss condition, and color ASCII graphics. I loved working in such an unfamiliar and challenging environment. There’s a serenity to making a game with such an uphill climb, because it took pressure off my mind to make the game impressive. Shipping anything written in assembly is impressive enough, I think.

I also:

  • Used Siri Shortcuts to parse a web endpoint for my local train times, and blogged about it.
  • Finished up my first LSP blog post
  • Made Hindsight, my “20 second break after 20 minutes of screen” app, a little smarter. Previously it checked if the Zoom app was running to avoid interrupting me in a call; now it checks to see if my camera is on instead
  • Went down a rabbit hole with wasm and dynamic linking. I didn’t end up achieving what I wanted to, but I learned some things that will hopefully make for an interesting blog post soon
